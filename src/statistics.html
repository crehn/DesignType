Dimension Variation by Experience

This chart shows how the design type changes as one gains more work experience. For each dimension, e.g. simple-vs.powerful, the ratio of the two sides is shown. If, for example, the graph shows a ratio of 60% simple for those with 4-6 years work experience, this means that out of those 60% are simple and 40% are rather powerful.

Our observations:

  • You can see that most people who took part in the survey are technologic (opposite of robust). To a certain extent this might be the case because those people like trying out new stuff such as this questionnaire. But the older one gets the more one tends to robust...
  • Simple vs powerful is very balanced for those with no work experience. Abstract and pragmatic overweights slightly and the technologic part is very present. Young developers are hungry for new technologies.
  • After a while there is a clear tendency towards abstract and simple but simple similar to technologic that is always important lessens for those with more than 15 years experience while abstract remains high.
